I am a researcher and educator in international business and corporate sustainability. My research focuses on the intersection of global value chains, multinational enterprises, and sustainable development. I am particularly interested in how firms balance competitive pressures with sustainability commitments, the role of digital technologies in enabling sustainability, and the impact of top management team diversity on corporate social responsibility.

Research Focus: Global Value Chains • Corporate Sustainability & CSR • UN Sustainable Development Goals • Multinational Enterprises • Top Management Team Diversity • Digital Technologies & Sustainability

Teaching

I teach courses at the graduate and undergraduate levels, with a focus on international business strategy, sustainability, and research methods. Since 2021, I have taught 11 distinct courses to approximately 560 students, offering 50.5 ECTS credits across multiple educational formats including semester courses, intensive international programs, and thesis coaching.

Current courses: International Business Applications (BSc), Global Strategy and International Alliances (BSc), International Business Strategy (International Programmes), Master's Thesis Coaching (MSc), and multiple international summer programmes.
